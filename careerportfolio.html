Career Research: Human-Centered Computing

A Focus on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Ethical AI

The long-term outlook for AI/ML engineers specializing in accessibility and user experience (UX) is exceptionally strong, projected to grow by over 30 percent by 2030 (Source 1). This niche addresses the vast market gap where technology fails to meet the needs of diverse cognitive styles.

  • Required Education: Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, often followed by a Master’s specializing in HCI or AI/ML.
  • Typical Responsibilities: Conducting rigorous user studies (like my work with neurodiverse students), designing and testing adaptive algorithms, and ensuring compliance with web accessibility standards (WCAG).
  • Potential Challenges: Navigating the ethical pitfalls of large language models (LLMs) and ensuring data privacy, which requires a strong foundation in digital ethics.
